# Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS) Appeal and Reconsideration Process

## Standard appeal method
- BCBS is a federation of independent plans (e.g., Anthem BCBS, Horizon BCBS). **Appeal process and timelines vary by plan and state.**
- Generally: submit a **written appeal** to the address on the EOB or through the plan’s provider portal.
- Typical deadline: **180 days** from date of denial for commercial; Medicare Advantage follows CMS rules (e.g., 60 days for reconsideration).

## Reconsideration vs appeal
- **Reconsideration**: For billing/coding corrections or missing information; often via portal or corrected claim.
- **Appeal**: For medical necessity, coverage, or authorization; formal written appeal with clinical documentation.

## What to include
- Member ID, group number, claim number, date of service.
- Denial code (CARC/RARC) and reason from EOB.
- Clinical justification: progress notes, treatment plan, relevant guidelines or LCD/NCD references for Medicare.
- For prior authorization: retro auth request, referral, and documentation supporting necessity.

## Official resources (verify before submission)
- Association hub for providers: [bcbs.com — providers](https://www.bcbs.com/providers)
- BCBS plans are local; use the EOB or member card for the correct portal. Sample clinical-editing PDF (Michigan): `payor_forms_downloads/BCBSM_Clinical_Editing_Reconsideration_Sample.pdf`

## Payer-specific notes
- Check the **specific BCBS plan** (e.g., state and product) for exact appeal address and forms.
- Many BCBS plans use **Premera**, **CareFirst**, or local plan names; use the correct portal or mailing address.
- Reference plan medical policies and, for Medicare, LCD/NCD when appealing medical necessity.
