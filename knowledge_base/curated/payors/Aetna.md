# Aetna Appeal and Reconsideration Process

## Standard appeal method
- Submit a **formal appeal** in writing. Use Aetna’s standard appeal form when required by the plan.
- **Timeline**: First-level appeal generally must be filed within **180 days** of the denial notice (check your plan document).
- **Where to submit**: Use the address or portal indicated on the EOB/denial letter. Commercial and Medicare plans may have different portals (e.g., Aetna Better Health for Medicaid).

## Reconsideration vs appeal
- **Reconsideration**: Often used for coding or billing corrections; may be faster and submitted via provider portal.
- **Appeal**: Full appeal for medical necessity, coverage, or authorization denials; follow the formal appeal process and attach clinical documentation.

## What to include
- Patient name, member ID, date of service, claim number.
- Denial code (CARC/RARC) and reason from EOB.
- Clinical justification and medical necessity (progress notes, treatment plan, relevant guidelines).
- For authorization denials: retro authorization request with ordering provider details and supporting records.

## Official resources (verify before submission)
- Provider appeals overview: [aetna.com — healthcare professionals, disputes and appeals](https://www.aetna.com/health-care-professionals/disputes-appeals/provider-appeals.html)
- PDF copies in-repo: `payor_forms_downloads/Aetna_Provider_Complaint_And_Appeal_Request.pdf`

## Payer-specific notes
- Reference Aetna medical policies (Clinical Policy Bulletins) when appealing medical necessity.
- For CPT/ICD issues, correct the claim and resubmit when appropriate; otherwise appeal with documentation supporting the codes used.
- Keep a copy of the submitted appeal and proof of submission (portal confirmation or certified mail).
