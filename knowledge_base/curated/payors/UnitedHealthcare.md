# UnitedHealthcare (UHC) Appeal and Reconsideration Process

## Standard appeal method
- **Commercial**: Submit appeal via UHC Provider Portal or in writing to the address on the EOB. Time limit is typically **180 days** from denial.
- **Medicare Advantage**: Follow CMS appeal rules; first level is plan reconsideration, then IRE. Deadlines are strict (e.g., 60 days for reconsideration).
- **Medicaid**: Follow state-specific process; often shorter timelines (e.g., 30–90 days).

## Reconsideration vs appeal
- **Reconsideration**: Used for clerical or billing errors; often done through portal or phone.
- **Appeal**: Formal appeal for medical necessity, coverage, or prior authorization; must be in writing with supporting documentation.

## What to include
- Member ID, claim number, date of service, CPT/ICD codes.
- Denial code and exact reason from remittance advice.
- Medical necessity: clinical notes, treatment plan, lab/imaging results, and reference to UHC medical policies or evidence-based guidelines.
- For prior auth denials: retro auth request, referral, and documentation showing necessity.

## Official resources (verify before submission)
- Claims, appeals, reconsiderations: [UHCprovider.com — appeals](https://www.uhcprovider.com/en/claims-payments-billing/appeals.html)
- Reconsideration PDF in-repo: `payor_forms_downloads/UnitedHealthcare_Claim_Reconsideration_Form.pdf`

## Payer-specific notes
- UHC publishes Medical Policy and Coverage Determination Guidelines; cite these when they support coverage.
- For bundled or NCCI-related denials, include correct coding and NCCI rationale or documentation for unbundling.
- Medicare Advantage appeals have specific timeframes and levels; document submission dates.
