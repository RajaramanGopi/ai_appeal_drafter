# Cigna Appeal and Reconsideration Process

## Standard appeal method
- Submit a **written appeal** using the address or method indicated on the denial/EOB. Commercial appeals are typically due within **180 days** of the denial.
- Provider portal may offer an electronic appeal option; confirm with the plan.

## Reconsideration vs appeal
- **Reconsideration**: For clerical or billing errors; may be handled via portal or corrected claim.
- **Appeal**: For medical necessity, coverage, or authorization denials; formal appeal with cover letter and supporting documentation.

## What to include
- Member ID, claim number, date of service, CPT and ICD codes.
- Denial code and reason from the remittance advice.
- Medical necessity: clinical notes, treatment plan, and reference to Cigna clinical guidelines or policy bulletins when applicable.
- For authorization denials: retro authorization request with ordering provider information and supporting medical records.

## Official resources (verify before submission)
- Appeals for healthcare professionals: [cigna.com — appeals and disputes](https://www.cigna.com/health-care-providers/coverage-and-claims/appeals-disputes)
- PDF in-repo: `payor_forms_downloads/Cigna_Request_For_Health_Care_Professional_Payment_Review.pdf`

## Payer-specific notes
- Cigna publishes coverage policies and clinical guidelines; cite them when they support the service.
- For coding denials (e.g., bundling, invalid CPT), correct the claim and resubmit if appropriate, or appeal with documentation justifying the codes.
- Keep proof of submission and a copy of the appeal package.
