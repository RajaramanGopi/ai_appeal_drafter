# Medicare (CMS) Appeal and Reconsideration Process

## Standard appeal method
- **Part A (institutional)**: Redetermination by Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC), then reconsideration by Qualified Independent Contractor (QIC), then ALJ, etc.
- **Part B (physician/supplier)**: Same progression—**Redetermination** (MAC) within 120 days, then **Reconsideration** (QIC) within 180 days of redetermination.
- **Medicare Advantage**: First level is plan **reconsideration** (typically 60 days); then IRE (Independent Review Entity), ALJ, etc.

## Reconsideration vs appeal
- **Redetermination**: First level for Part A/B; request in writing to the MAC with evidence.
- **Reconsideration**: Second level (QIC for Part A/B; plan reconsideration for MA). Must include all evidence; new evidence allowed at reconsideration for Part A/B in many cases.

## What to include
- Beneficiary name, Medicare ID (HICN/MBI), claim number, date of service.
- Denial reason and any denial code from the MSN or remittance.
- Clinical documentation supporting medical necessity; reference **LCD (Local Coverage Determination)** or **NCD (National Coverage Determination)** when applicable.
- For prior authorization issues: documentation that supports the service and any applicable coverage policy.

## Official resources (verify before submission)
- First-level (redetermination) overview: [CMS — first level appeal](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/appeals-grievances/fee-for-service/first-level-appeal-redetermination-medicare-contractor)

## Payer-specific notes
- CMS and MACs publish LCDs and NCDs; appeals that cite the correct policy and show the service meets criteria have better outcomes.
- Timely filing: provide proof of timely submission (e.g., clearinghouse report) if denial was for late filing.
- Medicare Advantage plans must follow CMS appeal regulations; deadlines are strict—document all submission dates.
