# CMS Coverage and Medical Necessity Guidelines (Summary)

## General principles
- Medicare covers services that are **reasonable and necessary** for diagnosis or treatment (Social Security Act § 1862(a)(1)(A)).
- Coverage is guided by **National Coverage Determinations (NCDs)** and **Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs)**. When no NCD/LCD applies, contractors use clinical literature and standards of practice.

## NCDs and LCDs
- **NCD**: National policy; same for all Medicare. Check CMS NCD list and policy text for indications and limitations.
- **LCD**: Set by Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs); can vary by region. LCDs describe covered diagnoses, CPT/HCPCS, and documentation requirements.
- When appealing, cite the **applicable LCD or NCD** and show that the patient meets the coverage criteria (diagnosis, documentation, and service type).

## Documentation requirements
- Medical records must support the level of service (e.g., E/M level) and medical necessity.
- Progress notes should include: history, examination, medical decision-making (or time); diagnosis; and plan.
- For procedures: document indication, consent, and outcome as required by policy.

## Common denial reasons and appeal approach
- **Medical necessity**: Submit clinical documentation that aligns with the LCD/NCD coverage criteria and explains why the service was needed.
- **Authorization**: For services that require prior approval, submit retro authorization request with supporting documentation; cite policy when prior auth was not required or was obtained.
- **Timely filing**: Provide proof of timely submission (e.g., clearinghouse confirmation, date of first submission).
